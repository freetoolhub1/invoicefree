10 Invoicing Mistakes Freelancers Make (And How to Fix Them)

By InvoiceFree.info  ·  January 20, 2025  ·  8 min read

Invoicing mistakes cost freelancers time, money, and clients. Here are the 10 most common errors — and exactly how to fix them using a professional invoice generator.

1. Not Including an Invoice Number

Every invoice needs a unique number. It helps with bookkeeping, tax filing, and client communication. InvoiceFree.info auto-generates sequential invoice numbers (INV-001, INV-002...) so you never forget.

2. Vague Payment Terms

Saying "payment due soon" is not a payment term. Use specific terms: Net 7, Net 15, Net 30, or "Due on Receipt". Our invoice generator has a built-in payment terms selector.

3. Missing Your Own Contact Information

Clients need to know who to pay and how to contact you with questions. Always include your email, phone, and business address.

4. Not Following Up on Late Invoices

Research shows that 29% of invoices are paid late. Set up a reminder system — send a polite follow-up 3 days after the due date, then another at 7 days.

5. Using a Generic PDF Filename

Name your invoice files descriptively: "Invoice_ClientName_2025-01.pdf" instead of "invoice.pdf". It's easier for clients to find and prevents confusion.

Did you know? Freelancers who send invoices within 24 hours of completing work get paid 2x faster than those who wait. Use InvoiceFree.info to create and send invoices immediately while the project is fresh.

6. Not Adding Your Logo

Branded invoices get paid 32% faster than plain text invoices, according to FreshBooks research. Upload your logo to make every invoice look polished and professional.

7. Wrong Currency

Always double-check the currency, especially for international clients. InvoiceFree.info supports 8 currencies including USD, EUR, GBP, PKR, INR, and AED.

8. Not Keeping Invoice Records

Your invoice history is your financial record. InvoiceFree.info automatically saves all your invoices locally so you always have a record.

9. Sending Without Proofreading

A typo in the total amount or client name looks unprofessional. Always preview your invoice before sending.

10. Not Including Tax Information

Depending on your location, you may need to include VAT, GST, or sales tax. InvoiceFree.info lets you set custom tax rates per line item.

How to Invoice International Clients: Multi-Currency Guide for Freelancers

By InvoiceFree.info  ·  February 10, 2025  ·  5 min read

Working with international clients is more common than ever. But invoicing across currencies, tax systems, and payment methods can be confusing. Here's the complete guide.

Which Currency Should You Invoice In?

The general rule: invoice in the client's currency if they're a large company, or your local currency if you're the smaller party. USD is universally accepted for international work.

Supported Currencies on InvoiceFree.info

  • USD ($) — US Dollar (most common international)
  • EUR (€) — Euro (EU clients)
  • GBP (£) — British Pound
  • INR (₹) — Indian Rupee
  • PKR (₨) — Pakistani Rupee
  • AED (د.إ) — UAE Dirham
  • JPY (¥) — Japanese Yen
  • CAD (CA$) — Canadian Dollar

Tax Considerations for International Invoices

Cross-border B2B services are often zero-rated for VAT/GST (the client handles their own tax in their country). However, you should still include your own tax registration number on the invoice and mark it clearly.
